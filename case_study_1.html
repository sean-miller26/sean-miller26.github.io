Zooniverse Landing Page Refresh

Zooniverse, the world’s largest and most popular platform for people-powered research, needed a visual and functional overhaul of its homepage. As the first touchpoint for new users, the homepage was underselling the mission, confusing in its messaging, and missed critical conversion opportunities. With concurrent upgrades to user profile pages, we leveraged this moment to refresh the homepage—turning it into an engaging, educational, and motivating entry point.

Design Timeline

4 months

Team

1 designer, 2 engineers

Platform

Web & Mobile

Industry

Citizen Science

Role

As the Lead UX/UI Designer, I was responsible for driving the overall design vision and execution of the homepage redesign. I led UX research efforts to understand user behavior, needs, and pain points, which informed key design decisions. In addition, I also coordinated closely with developers, marketing, comms, and internal stakeholders to ensure a smooth handoff and cohesive implementation across teams.

Impact

This redesign is aimed at increased engagement, conversion boosts, and creating a homepage that better reflects the scale, purpose, and community of Zooniverse.

A thoughtfully designed experience can significantly increase clarity, build trust, and guide potential volunteers into meaningful participation. By clearly communicating the mission and showcasing real projects and people behind the science, the homepage becomes a powerful storytelling tool that draws users in emotionally and intellectually.

Problems

What is the Zooniverse?

Users arrive to the site expecting a quick understanding of purpose.

Impersonal tone

Humans connect through story, something that is wholly lacking.

No clear call to action

Why should a user explore more of the site? What potential is there?

Goals

Make Zooniverse immediately understandable and compelling to newcomers. We aimed to clearly communicate what the platform is, how it works, and why it matters—within seconds of landing on the page. This meant elevating core content like our introductory video, featuring active projects more prominently, and telling a more human, story-driven narrative through researcher profiles and real user contributions. The redesign also focused on improving usability across devices, particularly tablets, using bold visuals, intuitive layout, and gesture-friendly elements like horizontal scrolling.

Research

My research combined qualitative and quantitative methods to uncover key usability issues and content gaps on the existing homepage. I analyzed user flows, reviewed site analytics, and mapped out visitor behavior to understand where users dropped off or became confused. User personas were developed to reflect the needs of both potential volunteers and project builders. One surprising insight came from internal stakeholders: researchers and team members were logging out of their accounts just to access key homepage content—highlighting how essential and yet siloed this information was.

Testing

Our testing approach focused on rapid iteration and internal validation, addressing the most critical usability issues. We conducted internal testing sessions with cross-functional team members—including developers, researchers, and stakeholders—to identify bugs and validate interactive elements. We also applied usability heuristics and design psychology principles (e.g., recognition over recall, information scent) to guide refinements. While formal user testing was limited due to timeline constraints, we prioritized quick feedback loops to ensure the final design was intuitive, informative, and action-oriented.

Launch

We launched the redesigned homepage alongside the rollout of our new stats system, supported by clear messaging and coordinated communications to inform users both before and after the transition. This update was part of a broader infrastructure shift to a modern, more flexible front-end framework, allowing for improved performance, scalability, and design consistency across the platform.

Outcome

The updated layout and messaging provided a clearer understanding of Zooniverse’s mission, leading to increased exploration of projects and higher user retention. Featuring our introductory video more prominently boosted views and watch time, while showcasing researcher stories and active projects fostered greater emotional connection and credibility. The new design also enhanced usability across devices, improved SEO performance, and reinforced brand identity. Overall, the launch successfully transformed the homepage into a more engaging, informative, and action-driven gateway to the Zooniverse platform.

Lessons Learned

As a team, we learned the value of cross-functional alignment early and often—especially when a project touches multiple aspects of the platform, from content to infrastructure. By working closely with developers, marketing, and internal researchers, we ensured that design decisions were technically feasible, on-brand, and rooted in real user needs. We also realized how critical it is to balance storytelling with clarity; while we wanted to inspire users, we learned through feedback and testing that users first need to understand what Zooniverse is before they can feel motivated to participate.

This project helped us rethink how we communicate value across the platform and reinforced the importance of designing for both new and returning audiences.
